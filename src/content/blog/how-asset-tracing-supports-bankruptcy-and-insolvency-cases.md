<p>Bankruptcy and insolvency cases are often multifaceted, involving more than one interested party, multiple assets and complex financials. Uncovering an undisclosed or wrongfully transferred pot of money can be a game-changer for lenders, law firms and trustees fighting to maximize recovery and level the playing field. And this is why asset tracing is so critical. At Investigation Plus, we are here to make light work of such difficult situations with our expertise in asset tracing.</p>
<h3><strong>What is Asset Tracing?</strong></h3>
<p>Asset tracing is the process of identifying, locating and tracking assets owned by an individual or entity. It’s imperative to protect assets that may have been squirrelled away, transferred, or otherwise improperly diverted from creditors or legal obligations. Asset tracing provides the same transparency to funds as is sought in the wasteful use of assets.</p>
<h3><strong>The Importance of Asset Tracing in Bankruptcy Cases</strong></h3>
<p>Creditors are often left high and dry when a business or individual declares bankruptcy. Asset tracing helps identify:</p>
<ul>
<li>	Hidden or undisclosed assets</li>
<li>	Family and relationship transfers or shell companies</li>
<li>	International assets and accounts</li>
<li>	Misappropriated funds or fraudulent transfers</li>
</ul>
<p>By identifying these assets, a more equitable division of assets can be made by creditors and trustees, and individuals can be prevented from circumventing the law by hiding their assets.</p>
<h3><strong>Supporting Insolvency Practitioners</strong></h3>
<p>Asset tracing is an essential element of insolvency practitioner and liquidator services, enabling the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>	Evaluate the net worth of a bankrupt person or business.</li>
<li>	Reclaim assets to pay the creditors.</li>
<li>	Uncover fraudulent or preferential transfers that may run afoul of bankruptcy statutes.</li>
<li>	Compile court papers and proof for the court, as appropriate.</li>
</ul>
<p>Asset tracing provides a foundation of evidence to support claims and facilitate recoveries.</p>
<h3><strong>Techniques Used in Asset Tracing</strong></h3>
<p>Asset tracing is most effective when it combines classic investigative techniques with the latest technology. Common techniques include:</p>
<ul>
<li>	Summary Examining financial records, bank statements and tax returns</li>
<li>	The identification and examination of corporate ownerships and inter-corporate transactions</li>
<li>	Checking on real estate holdings and property registrations</li>
<li>	Pursuing Offshore Accounts and International Holdings</li>
<li>	Digital Forensics for Discovering Hidden Digital Assets</li>
</ul>
<p>At Investigation Plus Ltd., we can tailor these methods to meet the specific needs of your case, ensuring that no asset remains hidden.</p>
